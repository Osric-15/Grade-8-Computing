Chapter 5 - consequences and mentoring

The school has the authority to handle student-related cases in accordance with applicable policies and considerations, under the following conditions:

There are 3 types of offences

  1. Mild offence
  2. Moderate offence
  3. Heavy offence

Mild offence

Mild offenses from the first to the fourth instance are handled by the subject teacher or homeroom teacher. Starting from the fifth offense, the matter is handled by the Student Affairs department.

Handling mechanism

  • Teachers/staff/other parties report student violations using a red offense slip to the Homeroom Teacher.
  • Follow -up actions, including parental notification, are handled by the homeroom teacher.
Violation no Consequences
1-2 Verbal warning and issuance of a red offense slip by the teacher/staff.
3 Verbal warning, red offense slip, and assignment of community service by the homeroom teacher.
4-5 Verbal warning and red offense slip by teacher/staff.
6 Verbal warning, red offense slip, community service, and parental summons.
7-8 Verbal warning and red offense slip.
9 Verbal warning, red offense slip, and community service.
10-11 Verbal warning and red offense slip.
12-17 Verbal warning, red violation slips, community service,parent summons, and referral to the Vice Principal of Student Affairs for the issuance of Warning Letter 1.
18-21 Verbal warning, red violation slips, community service,parent summons, and referral to the Vice Principal of Student Affairs for the issuance of Warning Letter 2.
22 Verbal warning, red violation slips, community service,parent summons, and referral to the Vice Principal of Student Affairsfor the issuance of Warning Letter 3.

Moderate offence

The first instance of a moderate offense is handled by the subject or homeroom teacher. From the second offense onward, it is managed directly by Student Affairs.

Handling mechanism

  • Teachers/staff report the violation via an offense slip to Student Affairs.
  • Follow-up is handled by the Student Affairs department.
Violation no Consequences
1 Verbal warning.
2 Notification letter and agreement letter
3 Parental summons and issuance of a second formal warning letter (maximum).

Additional Consequences:

    Deduction of discipline score on the report card Assigned tasks in the library and dismissal at 16:00 for 10 active school days

Heavy offence

Severe offenses are handled directly by the Student Affairs department starting from the first occurrence and so on.

Handling mechanism

  • The violation is reported using an offense slip by any teacher/staff member to Student Affairs.
  • Follow-up actions are handled by the Student Affairs department.
Violation no Consequences
1 Issuance of a third and final warning letter and the student is returned to their parents.

Mentoring

  1. Regular reporting to the Vice Principal of Student Affairs
  2. Mentoring by the homeroom teacher/mentor
  3. Counselling by the school counsellor
